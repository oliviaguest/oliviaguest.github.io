Looking...
but not respectfully

{% if page.date %}Olivia Guest, {{ page.date | date: "%e %B %Y" }}{% endif %}

On some level, when I moved to the Netherlands, I moved to the nowhere in the expression "the view from nowhere". Many seem not only entitled, but openly willing to take time out to share how they see me in ways (or perhaps severity) I had never experienced before. But academic and cultural shocks aside — I also wes deemed not a native English speaker as a child, as well as officially deemed a different ethnicity to my mother as back then it was legally patrilineal — I am used to the disrespectful eye from nowhere.

However, it not only looks at you, its perspective is self-reifying. And it talks! It narrates how it sees you. And it dominates you — the eye's view is the view. Other views are merely derivative, definable only through first traveling to nowhere, empathizing with nowhere, and framing everything you saw for yourself somewhere with respect to nowhere. Vacuous inert facile boring nowhere.

You must first hear from nowhere — think about nowhere — before you can even talk about your own somewhere. You must become an expert on nowhere, psychologize nowhere, respect nowhere, accept nowhere. The internal monologue of this denizen of nowhere, or what should be left as their inner thoughts, is running commentary on your life. Their stream of consciousness has to intrude on your stream of consciousness... or else!

"Or else what?" you might think if this is not familiar to you. And I would say "or else you are not able to speak yourself!" You can only reply to the eye; you cannot just speak. Everything you have to say can only be understood if framed with respect to what the eye already could see before it specifically looked at you.

This is called epistemic violence, if you are forced through misunderstanding or otherwise to waste your time explaining yourself, or epistemic injustice, for the general case of this view from nowhere situation. And it is violent because we're not from nowhere; and those who think they are may have lost a part of themselves in the process of, to put it bluntly, accommodating white and male supremacy.
