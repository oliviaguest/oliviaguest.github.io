Looking...
but not respectfully

{% if page.date %}Olivia Guest, {{ page.date | date: "%e %B %Y" }}{% endif %}

When I moved to the Netherlands, I think I moved to the nowhere in the expression "the view from nowhere". Many seem openly willing to share how they see me in ways I had never experienced before — or not before in such severity, confidence, and with no respite.

I decided against listing personal examples from childhood to now and I certainly won't even mention specific anecdotes from the professional sphere. More than anything, I don't want to make this so much about me as about this eye's effect on my and others' work. But trust me, I am used to the disrespectful eye from nowhere, which by the way can grow on anybody's face, especially these days.

However, the eye not only looks at you, because its perspective is self-reifying. It dominates you. The eye's view is the view. Other views are merely derivative, definable only through first traveling to nowhere, empathizing with nowhere, and framing everything you saw for yourself somewhere with respect to nowhere. Vacuous facile boring nowhere.

You must first hear from nowhere — think about nowhere — before you can even talk about your own somewhere. If you don't all with such an eye ignore you — and it's a performative shunning as well as everything else.

None of these are unique realisations of course, they are at least as old as me, if not older: epistemic violence coined by Gayatri Spivak and Miranda Fricker's epistemic injustice. We know nothing because we're not originally or otherwise from nowhere.

When we are forced through misunderstanding or otherwise to waste our time explaining ourselves, we can lose a part of ourselves. And those who think they are from nowhere originally sadly, may have never had a chance to grow those parts of their humanity.

The eye isn't an eye, by the way... it's a machine. A towering edifice of people, culture, coercion, domination, which hangs over us. And not only does it chew us up, for many of us it also feeds us. For academics, even for academics who notice its glare (let alone react to the gears it crushes us under) we are paid handsomely. Are we all part of it? Probably. Some are fuel, consumed by definition, and others are pistons, loyal components replaced in due course through normal wear and tear — and some are in the driver's seat, with human dignity and structural protections. These protections and benefits are provided to them by the rest of the machine: people they gladly see as equals when needed, and then happily turn a blind eye to when "things don't make sense".

"The Lidless Eye"

So far so serious, and maybe even unimaginably sad Unimaginably because as far as I can tell it is out of grasp to those who don't understand what it means to be subjected to the eye's gaze. A whole culture denoted as inferior or non-existent is ineffable. And certainly, some know this even more than me, but I also have been everything from bedevilled to disabled by nowhere since 2020.

Surprisingly, I have a lighter point. One of the ways in which my work as a cognitive scientist is misunderstood and erased is because the eye only wants to look through the lens of contemporary artificial intelligence and related ontological confusions on models and theory. Let me explain.

What I won't do is tell you what the eye sees. Like I said that's boring as hell, maybe even hell itself. And it's easy to find everywhere within the field. I will tell you what the eye has or will extract, inadvertently, because regardless of what it sees, it knows how to take it.
